### Version 0.1

**Effective date:** To be set at first public launch

---

## Why these guidelines exist

Harbor is built on a simple premise: your time and attention have value, and a social platform should earn them honestly.

These guidelines exist to keep Harbor a place where that is actually possible — where you can catch up, connect, learn, and create without being subjected to abuse, manipulation, or content designed to harm. We moderate because moderation protects people. We publish these guidelines because you deserve to know exactly what the rules are and why.

We will never take a meaningful action against your account or content without telling you clearly what happened, which rule it violated, and how to appeal.

---

## Scope

These guidelines apply to all content and behavior on Harbor, including posts, replies, profiles, groups, and messaging behavior. Direct messages on Harbor are end-to-end encrypted — we cannot read them. We enforce messaging-related guidelines through rate limits, account patterns, and reports you submit to us voluntarily.

---

## Minimum age

Harbor is for people 13 and older. Accounts we believe belong to someone under 13 may be suspended or removed. In some regions, the minimum age is higher due to local law — we comply with those requirements.

We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has created an account, please contact us at policy@joinharbor.app.

## What isn't allowed

### Harassment

Content or behavior targeting a person to intimidate, demean, or harm them.

**This includes:**
- Directed abuse, threats, or slurs targeting an individual
- Inciting others to harass someone ("go tell them…")
- Coordinated pile-ons — we look at intent, targeting, hostility, and how fast it's spreading, not just the number of replies
- Doxxing: sharing someone's private information (home address, phone number, workplace, location patterns) without their consent
- Sexualized harassment or sexualized targeting of a person
- Stalking: repeated unwanted contact, especially after being blocked
- Creating new accounts to continue harassment after an enforcement action

**This is not harassment:**
Criticism of ideas, public figures, institutions, or creative works — even harsh criticism — is not harassment. Disagreement, satire, and protest are protected. We distinguish between targeting a person and engaging with their ideas.

---

### Hate and hateful conduct

Content that attacks or dehumanizes people based on who they are — including race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, or caste.

**This includes:**
- Slurs used to attack a group
- Content that dehumanizes people ("vermin," "subhuman," or equivalent framing)
- Calls to exclude, expel, or deny rights to people based on protected characteristics
- Symbols or imagery used to promote hatred (we look at context and intent)

**This is not hateful conduct:**
Discussing, analyzing, or countering discrimination and hate movements is not a violation. Disagreement with a group's beliefs or practices is not hate.

---

### Spam and inauthentic behavior

Coordinated or automated activity that pollutes the information environment or deceives people about the source or scale of content.

**This includes:**
- Fake accounts, bot networks, and coordinated inauthentic amplification
- Unsolicited bulk messaging
- Repeatedly posting the same content to game distribution
- Impersonating another person or organization
- Engagement bait designed to game distribution rather than start genuine conversation
- Links that deliver malware or steal credentials

---

### Graphic content

Content depicting real-world violence, gore, or serious injury in a gratuitous way that serves no informational or documentary purpose.

**This includes:**
- Graphic violence or gore without documentary context
- Animal cruelty or torture

Content with genuine news or documentary value may be labeled and distribution-restricted rather than removed. We do not recommend this content in discovery surfaces regardless.

*Sexual content is covered separately in the next section.*

---

### Sexual content and nudity

Harbor is for a 13+ audience. We do not allow pornography or sexually explicit content.

**Never allowed:**
- Pornography or explicit sexual acts
- Nudity in a sexual context or intended for sexual gratification
- Sexual solicitation or offers or requests for sexual acts
- Non-consensual sexual content, hidden-camera sexual content, or sexual deepfakes
- Sexually predatory behavior or exploitation

**Generally not allowed:**
- Full nudity

**Limited exceptions** (allowed with a label and distribution restrictions):
- Breastfeeding and childbirth
- Non-sexual educational, documentary, scientific, medical, or artistic content where nudity is not gratuitous

We do not recommend sexual content in any discovery surface. Attempts to use Harbor to distribute sexual content result in content removal and account-level action.

---

### Illegal content

Content that is illegal regardless of context.

**This includes:**
- Child sexual abuse material — removed immediately and reported to authorities where required by law
- Content facilitating human trafficking
- Content that directly facilitates serious violence or constitutes credible incitement to violence
- Facilitation of the sale of illegal goods or services where prohibited by law

---

### Scams, fraud, and phishing

Deliberate deception designed to steal from people.

**This includes:**
- Phishing: fake login pages, credential harvesting, account takeover attempts
- Investment fraud and "get rich" schemes
- Impersonating an organization or individual to solicit money or information
- Links or content that deliver malware or enable unauthorized access

---

### Misleading content

Deliberate deception with a plausible path to real-world harm.

**This includes:**
- Impersonating a real person or organization with intent to deceive
- Manipulated media (deepfakes, deceptive edits) presented as authentic without disclosure
- Health or safety misinformation where the deception could cause physical harm
- Satire that is not reasonably identifiable as satire — in civic contexts, labeling may be required

**Our approach:**
When possible, we add context — labels, warnings, sharing friction — rather than removing content. When deception creates a meaningful risk of real-world harm, we remove or restrict distribution.

**This is not misleading content:**
Opinion, clearly labeled satire, speculation, and good-faith disagreement about contested facts are not violations.

---

### Privacy

Sharing private information about people without their consent.

**This includes:**
- Sharing someone's private identifying information without consent — even when it is technically findable
- Non-consensual intimate imagery: sharing sexual or intimate images of a person without their consent
- Sharing private messages or conversations without the consent of the people involved
- Publishing location or movement information in ways that enable stalking

---

### Self-harm

Content that encourages, instructs, or glorifies self-harm or suicide.

**This includes:**
- Encouraging or inciting self-harm or suicide directed at another person
- Detailed instructions for methods of self-harm
- Glorification of self-harm or eating disorders

Content discussing self-harm in a recovery, awareness, or support context is not a violation. We may add crisis resource information to content that touches on these topics in ambiguous ways. If you or someone you know needs help, please reach out to a crisis service in your area.

---

### Child safety

Harbor has zero tolerance for content or behavior that endangers minors.

**This includes:**
- Any sexual content involving minors — removed immediately and reported where required by law
- Grooming: adults building inappropriate relationships with minors to exploit them
- Attempts to move minors to off-platform channels for exploitation
- Coercion, blackmail, or threats involving minors

We provide in-app reporting tools and take urgent action on all credible child safety reports.

---

### Manipulation

Coordinated campaigns designed to artificially distort what people see or experience on Harbor.

**This includes:**
- Networks of accounts acting together to inflate perceived support for content or ideas
- Mass-reporting content or accounts to abuse our enforcement systems
- Coordinated harassment disguised as civic accountability
- Attempting to game Harbor's systems to unfairly throttle someone else's content
- Hidden coordination presented as organic activity

## How enforcement works

### The actions we take

We use the least restrictive action that addresses the harm. In order from least to most restrictive:

**On content:**
1. **Label** — a contextual note or warning shown to viewers
2. **Interstitial** — a click-through warning required before content is shown
3. **Distribution limits** — content is no longer eligible for discovery or sharing features; this is not a shadow ban — if we limit distribution due to policy, we tell you
4. **Removal**

**On accounts:**
1. **Feature limits** — specific capabilities temporarily restricted (for example: broadcast sharing disabled, or messages limited to requests only)
2. **Temporary suspension**
3. **Permanent removal** — for severe violations, repeated violations after prior enforcement, or zero-tolerance categories like child safety

### We tell you

Every action that meaningfully limits your content's reach or restricts your account generates a notice — specific, in plain language, citing the relevant section of these guidelines. We do not silently reduce your distribution and pretend nothing happened.

In rare cases where telling you immediately would allow someone to evade an active investigation — for example, a bot network disruption — we may delay a notice briefly. We send it as soon as it is safe to do so.

### Ranking vs. enforcement

A post can be less relevant to a particular user's feed without that being an enforcement action. If your reach is limited because of a policy decision, we will say so. If your content simply isn't the right fit for a particular intent or audience, that is the algorithm doing its job — not a penalty. Harbor keeps these two things separate and auditable.

### What every notice includes

- What action was taken
- Which content it applies to
- Which guideline was triggered and what specifically caused it
- How to appeal and what information helps your case

### Appeals

Every enforcement action that removes content or meaningfully limits reach includes an appeal path. You can submit an appeal from the Notices section of your profile or by emailing appeals@joinharbor.app.

Most appeals are reviewed within 72 hours. When your appeal is decided, you will receive a follow-up with the outcome and a specific reason — not a form letter. We publish aggregate appeal and overturn rates in our transparency reports.

---

## What we will never do

- **Shadow ban.** If your content's reach is limited, you will know about it.
- **Act without a reason.** Every enforcement action cites a specific guideline section. "Violation of community standards" with no further explanation is not acceptable here.
- **Punish disagreement.** Viewpoint diversity is a goal at Harbor, not a threat. Content that challenges consensus, criticizes institutions, or expresses minority views is not a moderation target.
- **Use moderation as a ranking tool.** Enforcement and ranking are separate systems. We keep them that way.
- **Read your messages.** Direct messages on Harbor are end-to-end encrypted. We cannot read them, and we will not try.
